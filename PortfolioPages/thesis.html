Who Stays, What Grows: An Analysis of the Homeownership and Tree Survivorship in Worcester, MA, between 2010 and 2021

Using field and vector data to analyze how home sales relate to tree canopy change and survivorship

Team Members:

Aaron Richmond-Crosset

Affiliation:

Research under the supervision of Professor John Rogan

Project Description:

This thesis project analyzed how homeownership patterns influence tree canopy change and survivorship in Worcester, Massachusetts. Using DCR planting data, property sales, and multi-year canopy datasets, the study compared sold and unsold parcels between 2010 and 2021. Results showed that trees on unsold parcels had higher survivorship and less canopy loss than those on sold parcels. These findings highlight the connection between residential stability, long-term tree care, and the success of urban planting programs.
