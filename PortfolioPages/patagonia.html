Determining suitable protection areas for Guanaco (Lama guanicoe) in Patagonia

Using Maxent species distribution modeling and anthropogenic risk analysis

Team Members:

Aaron Richmond-Crosset, Ben Gaskill, Philipp Steinhilber

Affiliation:

GEOG 397: Advanced Raster GIS

Project Description:

This project identified suitable protection areas for the Guanaco (Lama guanicoe) across Patagonia using species presence data, environmental variables, and spatial modeling in TerrSet. Employing the MAXENT algorithm, the study generated a habitat suitability map and assessed how well current protected areas align with optimal Guanaco habitats. Results revealed that most high-suitability regions lie in eastern Patagonia, while existing reserves are concentrated in the west. By integrating anthropogenic risk factors such as roads and settlements, the analysis highlighted priority areas where conservation efforts should focus to protect the species under increasing human pressure.
