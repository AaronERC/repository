Assessing Accessibility to Maternal Health Facilities in Turkana County, Kenya

Using a Suitiblity Model in ArcPro

Team Members:

Aaron Richmond-Crosset

Affiliation:

SSJ 306: GIS for International Development

Project Description:

This project assessed spatial accessibility to maternal health facilities in Turkana County, Kenya, using a weighted GIS model incorporating population density, road networks, terrain, and land cover. Results revealed significant disparities, with central and southern regions having better access, while northern and eastern areas—characterized by difficult terrain and limited infrastructure—remain highly underserved. The analysis identifies priority zones for deploying mobile health services or constructing new facilities and highlights the critical need for improved infrastructure and data quality to support maternal health equity in remote regions.
