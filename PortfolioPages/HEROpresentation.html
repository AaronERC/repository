Analysis of Tree Health and Structure in the Worcester, MA Longhorned Beetle Regulation Zone

Data collected with ArcGIS Field Maps, Analysis and visualizations done in R

Team Members:

Aaron Richmond-Crosset, Adlai Nelson, Amritha Pai, Caleb Kluchman, Ksenia Smart, Ramón Colón, Tanner Honnef

Affiliation:

Human Environment Research Observatory

Project Description:

This project assessed the long-term outcomes of a tree replanting program in Worcester, MA, following the Longhorned Beetle infestation. Using field surveys of 3,000 trees and 52 resident interviews, the study evaluated tree survivorship, growth, and stewardship practices. Results showed 66.9% survivorship for private trees and 88.6% for street trees, with species like linden and honeylocust performing best. Interviews revealed strong resident appreciation for shade and aesthetics, but also highlighted maintenance challenges. The findings underscore the importance of species selection, early watering, and community engagement for successful urban reforestation.
