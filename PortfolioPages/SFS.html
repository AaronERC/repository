Assessing the Effectiveness of the Mbirikani Predator Consolation Fund on Mitigating Human-Lion Conflict

Using household surveys and key informant interviews

Team Members:

Aaron Richmond-Crosset, Alexandra Greene, Pranneil Thankavel

Affiliation:

Research under the supervision of Professor Dr. Richard Kiaka

Project Description:

This project evaluated the effectiveness of the Mbirikani Predator Compensation Fund in promoting human–lion coexistence in the Amboseli-Tsavo ecosystem. Using household surveys and key informant interviews, the study examined community perceptions of the program’s fairness, efficiency, and impact on retaliatory lion killings. Results showed that while the fund reduced conflict and improved tolerance toward predators, challenges in payment delays and livestock valuation affected satisfaction. These findings underscore the importance of timely compensation and community trust in sustaining conservation initiatives
